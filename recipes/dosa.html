Dosa

Description

Dosa is a thin, crispy Indian pancake made from fermented rice and lentil batter.

It is often served with a variety of chutneys and sambar, making it a versatile and flavorful dish.

Its golden-brown texture and savory taste make dosa a popular choice for breakfast or any meal of the day.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Urad dal (black gram split lentils)
  • Fenugreek seeds
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)

Steps

  1. Rinse and soak the rice and urad dal (along with fenugreek seeds) in water for about 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them together with a little water to form a smooth, thick batter. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours.
  3. Once fermented, stir the batter and add salt to taste.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or dosa pan over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it out in a thin, circular motion to form a dosa.
  6. Cook until the edges start to lift and the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Optionally, you can drizzle a bit of oil or ghee around the edges for extra crispiness.
  7. Flip the dosa and cook for an additional minute if you prefer a crispier texture.
  8. Remove the dosa from the pan and serve hot with chutneys and sambar.
